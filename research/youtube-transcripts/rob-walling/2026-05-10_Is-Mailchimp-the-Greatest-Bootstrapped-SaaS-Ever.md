If you're building something on your own or considering building something, what can we actually take away from Ben and Dan's story? First, your side project might be the thing. Mailchimp was an afterthought for nine years. Ben and Dan kept treating the design agency as the real business. Sometimes the thing that's paying for lunch while you focus on your real work turns out to be the big play. Pay attention to what's working even if it's not what you planned. Second, constraints can create breakthroughs. Freemium happened because a programmer couldn't ship on time. The lack of VC funding forced them to stay profitable from day one. You can't plan for constraints to create breakthroughs, but you can choose not to optimize them away. Sometimes the limitation is the gift. Third, pick your mountain.
